When to choose the two-step correction

If light polishing barely scratches the surface of your defects, two-step gives you the cutting power and clarity you need. Look for these cues.

Sign 01

Moderate swirling + RIDS

Scratches visible at a distance or caused by improper washing demand a cut pass before refining.

Sign 02

Dealer-installed holograms

Those rotary buffer trails need a targeted cut to flatten, followed by a haze-removing finish.

Sign 03

Pre-coating perfection

Ceramic coatings lock in whatever is beneath. Two-step resets the paint so coatings perform.

Sign 04

Heavily oxidised paint

Older single-stage or neglected metallic paints need more bite plus a jewelling pass.

What Causes It

Defects are layered—two steps address each layer

Years of automatic washes, bird etching, tree sap and dealer prep marks etch deeply. A soft finishing pass alone won’t pull them out.

  • Improper compounding leaves heavy buffer trails that must be re-cut.
  • Environmental etching from bird droppings or sap penetrates deeper into the clear.
  • Neglected single-stage paint oxidises and requires more than a finesse polish.

Our Process

Two-step correction workflow

01

Prep & masking

Decon wash, clay, iron removal and extensive tape-up to protect trim, rubber and badges.

02

Cut stage

Heavy defect removal using measured passes, forced rotation tools and tailored compounds.

03

Refine & jewell

04

Protection & inspection

Panel wipe, sealant/coating prep and inspection under high-CRI lighting with you.
