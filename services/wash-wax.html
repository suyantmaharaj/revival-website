How to tell your vehicle is asking for a full Wash & Wax

A fast drive-through wash hides the true condition of the paint. These are the cues we look for before recommending this treatment.

Sign 01

Water no longer beads

When rinse water sheets across panels instead of forming tight beads, your last layer of protection has completely evaporated.

Sign 02

Paint feels rough after a wash

Embedded fallout and overspray make the surface grabby. That is when we introduce a safe decontamination step before waxing.

Sign 03

Lower panels look hazy

Road film, tar and Cape Town salt spray cling to rocker panels and doors, dulling metallic flake and showing uneven reflections.

Sign 04

Trim and badges trap grime

Hand contact areas collect oils that normal washes miss. Precision detailing brushes and compressed air release the build-up.

What Causes It

Everyday driving layers contaminants faster than you think

From Atlantic humidity to the inland dust belt, Cape Town’s climate is both coastal and desert in a single commute. That cocktail accelerates oxidation and strips cheap waxes within weeks.

  • Mineral-rich borehole water leaves alkaline spotting that etches clear coat if not neutralised.
  • Construction fallout embeds into horizontal surfaces, giving you that sandpaper feel.
  • UV + sea salt break down dealership waxes, exposing your paint to premature fading.

Our Process

Intentional steps for measurable gloss

01

Foam pre-wash & rinse

Loosens heavy grime while our detailers focus on sensitive areas like PPF edges, satin trims and matte badges.

02

Three-bucket contact wash

Dedicated wash buckets for paint, wheels and exhaust trims so nothing abrasive crosses over.

03

Decontamination & drying

Clay, iron remover and correct drying towels leave the paint absolutely flat and ready for protection.

04

Wax locking & inspection

Wax is applied and buffed before a co-signed inspection with you.
