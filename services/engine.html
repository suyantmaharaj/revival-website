How to tell your engine bay needs professional attention

Under the hood is the first place enthusiasts and buyers inspect. These cues mean it is time for Revival.

Sign 01

Oily film on plastics

Road grime and engine mist coat covers, giving them a dull grey look.

Sign 02

Leaves & debris collecting

Blocked gutters by the windscreen trap moisture, inviting corrosion and mouldy smells in the cabin.

Sign 03

Visible coolant / oil stains

Old spills attract dust and make leak detection impossible.

Sign 04

Crusty battery terminals

Acid residue eats clamps and impacts charging.

What Causes It

Cape Town conditions attack from underneath and above

Commuting through rain, dust and coastal air throws contaminants up into the bay and the heat bakes them on.

  • Sea salt spray rides under the bonnet and corrodes mounts and bolts.
  • Gravel road dust clings to damp hoses and wiring looms.
  • DIY dressings leave greasy residue that grabs dirt and risks electronics.

Revival mitigates this with

Contactless pre-rinse

Foam soaks and pressure-mapped rinses keep water away from sensitive components.

Brush + steam detailing

Soft brushes loosen grime in badges, strut braces and sensor housings.

Drying + dressing protocol

Compressed air blast, towel dry and OEM-safe dressing restores a uniform shine.

Our Process

Engine bay detail workflow

01

Prep & protection

Cover air intakes, alternators and exposed electronics, then document the bay before cleaning.

02

Degrease & agitate

Layered chemicals + APC foams, detail brushes and microfiber work dirt out of every contour.

03

Controlled rinse

Low-pressure rinse for tight areas, followed by compressed air drying to remove moisture.

04

Dressing & inspection

UV-safe dressing, metal polishing where possible, and before/after photos to sign off with you.
